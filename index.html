CleanClick

A neuromuscular surgical interface that converts subtle forearm muscle signals into direct control of medical imaging and digital systems—allowing surgeons to navigate data without breaking sterility.

Explore Technology Meet the Team

The Mission

CleanClick aims to restore digital autonomy to surgeons inside the operating room. While surgical technology has advanced rapidly, direct interaction with medical data is still constrained by sterile protocols.

By translating neuromuscular signals into digital commands, CleanClick allows surgeons to control imaging and software naturally, removing communication barriers and improving workflow efficiency in high‑stakes clinical environments.

The Challenge

During many complex procedures, surgeons must rely on assistants to manipulate imaging systems and patient data displayed on monitors.

These repeated communication loops interrupt concentration, introduce delays, and extend operating room time—one of the most expensive resources in modern healthcare.

The Solution

CleanClick is a wearable bio‑electronic interface that detects micro‑muscle activity using surface electromyography (sEMG). Even through surgical clothing, the device captures subtle forearm signals and converts them into precise digital commands.

This enables sterile, hands‑free interaction with imaging systems and medical software, allowing surgeons to remain fully focused on the procedure.

Project Summary

Developed at TU Eindhoven for the TU/e Contest, CleanClick explores how wearable neural interfaces can transform human‑computer interaction in operating rooms. The project integrates biomedical sensing, signal processing, and intuitive interface design to reduce workflow friction and enable faster clinical decision‑making.
